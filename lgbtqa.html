WELCOME

The City of Birmingham is committed to serving the LGBTQ+ community through uplift and empowerment. We stand in solidarity against all forms of discrimination and oppression against lesbian, gay, bisexual, trans and queer individuals who live, work and/or play in the City. As such, Birmingham offers employment benefits tailored to the needs of its LGBTQ+ employees and has a comprehensive set of non-discrimination laws, which prohibit sexual orientation and gender identity discrimination by business establishments, by commercial housing agents and by service providers in the City. We are determined to provide LGBTQ+ residents, workers and visitors in the City of Birmingham with an equitable and safe community that we all can call home.

Mayors LGBTQ Advisory board

In 2018, Mayor Randall Woodfin established the LGBTQ advisory board, a 28-member body composed of citywide LGBTQ+ leaders and advocates tasked with identifying solutions, making recommendations and helping shape the city's policies and engagements with Birmingham's LGBTQ+ communities.

Mayor Woodfin's LGBTQ+ Advisory Board exemplifies the diversity of the Birmingham LGBTQ+ community with White/Non-Hispanic, African American, Hispanic, Asian and biracial members; along with individuals from across the LGBTQ spectrum to include gay, lesbian, bisexual pansexual, queer, transgender, gender non-conforming and ally communities. Across two-year terms, the LGBTQ+ Advisory Board works to develop a healthy, thriving LGBTQ+ community focusing on aspects of health, legal, political, advocacy, faith and community.

Statewide LGBTQ+
Nondiscrimination laws


The state of Alabama along with 29 other states lack LGBTQ+ non-discrimination laws. In 2019, mayor Woodfin joined the Mayors Against LGBTQ+ Discrimination coalition along with 140 other mayors, city council members and municipal leaders to urge Congress to pass a comprehensive set of nondiscrimination protections at the federal level. Mayors against LGBTQ Discrimination is a bipartisan coalition of municipal leaders dedicated to securing inclusive nondiscrimination protections for all to ensure that every LGBTQ+ American is protected no matter what city or state they call home.

Birmingham Aids Outreach (BAO)

Birmingham AIDS Outreach and the Office of the Mayor have demonstrated the correlation between mental and physical health disparities for the LGBTQ+ population and have a commitment to work together to provide services to the LGBTQ+ community. The collaborative agreement includes providing support, hosting training classes, recieving referrals and referring LGBTQ+ individuals to BAO programs and community education.

HUMAN RIGHTS CAMPAIGN
MUNICIPAL EQUALITY INDEX

Birmingham earned over 100 points on the 2020 Municipal Equality Index (MEI), despite hailing from a state without statewide nondiscrimination statutes that explicitly include sexual orientation and gender identity. Across the country, municipalities like these set a standard of LGBTQ+ inclusiveness by prioritizing measures such as local nondiscrimination laws. Providing transgender-inclusive health benefits for city employees, and providing services for particularly vulnerable members of the LGBTQ community. Trailblazing the path forward for equality, Birmingham earned one of HRC's 61 MEI "All-Star" designations.
